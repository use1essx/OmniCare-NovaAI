# Response Guidelines (Motor Screening Skill)

- Keep language simple and clear; avoid medical jargon.
- Use short bullets/paragraphs that copy-paste cleanly into a case note.
- Mention the child’s age/context if provided (“For a 6-year-old…”).
- Be concise but specific about movement points (symmetry, balance, rhythm, effort).
- If info is insufficient, state what extra detail or footage is needed.
- End with an encouraging, non-judgmental line: “You’re doing the right thing by paying attention and asking questions.”
- Always restate that this is early screening only, not a diagnosis or treatment plan.
